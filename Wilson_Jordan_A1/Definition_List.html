Words and Definitions

Headings
A heading is what goes at the top of a web page and can look like, < h1 >, < h2 >... < h6 >. To close, you put a < /h1 >, < /h2 >... < /h6 > at the end.
Paragraphs
A group of sentences that form one idea. In HTML, paragraphs start with < p > and end with < /p >
Bold & Italic
Adds emphasis to words in a paragraph. Bold starts with < b > and ends with < /b >. Italics starts with < i > and ends with < /i >
Superscript & Subscript
Superscript looks like 4th and starts with < sup > and ends with < /sup >. Subscript looks like H20 and starts with < sub > and ends with < /sub >
White Space
Is the space(s) between words. Web browsers will collaps all white space so it looks like there is only one space
Line Breaks
Will take your text to the next line. it looks like < br / >
Horizontal Rules
It will draw a horizontal line across your screen. It looks like < hr / >
Visual Editors
Looks like a word processor but is optimized for coding
Semantic Markup
Adds extra information to your web page. < blockquote > adds quotation marks and < em > adds emphasis to a word
Strong Emphasis
Makes a word bold. Starts with < strong > and ends with < /strong >
Quotations
Adds quotation marks around some text. It starts with < q > and ends with < /q >
Abbreviations
A simplified spelling of a word. It looks like < abbr title="Professor" < Prof > </abbr >
Acronyms
Usually the first letter of each word to make a new meaning. It looks like < acronym title="National Aeronautics and Space Administration" < NASA > </acronym >
Citations & Definitions
Citations is where you get some information and looks like < cite >. and definitions is what a word means and looks like < dfn >
Author Details
Gives details of a person and looks like < address >
Changes to Content
Looks like a sale. Keeps the new deal and scratches out the old. It looks like < ins >, < del > and < s >
Ordered Lists
Gives a numbered list. Starts with < ol >, ends with < /ol > with < li > and < /li > in between
Unordered Lists
Gives a bulleted list. Starts with < ul >, ends with < /ul > with < li > and < /li > in between
Definition Lists
Gives structure a word with a definition. Starts with < dl >, ends with < /dl > with < dt > for the word and < dd > for the definition
Nested Lists
Is a list within a list
